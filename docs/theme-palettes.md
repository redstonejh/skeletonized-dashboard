# Theme Palettes

The dashboard palette system separates light/dark mode, accent color, and workspace background tone. Background palettes should shape the ambient workspace without changing widget accent identity or weakening panel readability.

## Light Palettes

- `warm-white`: Warm neutral base for calm everyday work. Best when the dashboard needs a soft paper-like atmosphere without pure white.
- `cool-white`: Cooler light workspace with a crisp productivity feel. Keep glass borders visible so surfaces do not disappear.
- `soft-grey`: Quiet neutral grey. Useful as the least opinionated light mode option.
- `cool-grey`: Grounded cool grey for modern productivity and design-tool workspaces. Richer than the default but still clearly light mode.
- `warm-grey`: Soft industrial warm grey. Use when a less blue workspace is desired while keeping comfortable contrast.
- `slate`: Darker light-mode slate with stronger surface separation. Good for dense dashboards that need less glare.
- `graphite-light`: Premium light graphite tone. This is a dark-light hybrid and should keep panel surfaces bright enough to float above the background.
- `light-blue-grey`: Restrained blue-grey. Softer than slate and suitable for cool neutral dashboards.
- `muted-blue-grey`: More grounded blue-grey with less pastel wash. Useful for glass UI that needs a cool workspace mood.
- `neutral-dim`: Warm neutral dim light mode. Avoids hospital-white glare while keeping text in the light contrast model.
- `stone-slate`: Balanced stone/cool slate mix. Good for soft industrial glass surfaces.
- `blue-mist`: Subtle blue mist. Keep this gentle and avoid saturated pastel overload.
- `frosted-light`: Default frosted neutral. It remains the safe balanced light preset.

## Dark Palettes

- `charcoal`: Default dark charcoal with calm contrast.
- `soft-charcoal`: Softer, lower-contrast charcoal for long work sessions.
- `graphite`: Lifted graphite tone with clear glass separation.
- `soft-black`: Deepest low-light option. Use strong enough surface and line tokens to prevent panels from flattening.
- `deep-navy`: Restrained navy, not saturated neon blue.
- `muted-navy`: Calmer navy with reduced blue intensity.
- `dark-blue-grey`: Neutral dark blue-grey with a cooler workspace feel.
- `deep-slate`: Cinematic deep slate with muted borders and low-neon contrast.
- `dark-glass`: Low-contrast dark glass base. Useful when chrome and panels should feel blended but still readable.
- `low-neon`: Dark cool workspace tuned to avoid electric blue/RGB edge lighting.
- `midnight-blue`: Deeper blue workspace. Keep glows restrained.
- `dark-frosted`: Default frosted dark neutral.

## Contrast Guidance

- Panels, widgets, nav chrome, popovers, and settings surfaces must remain visibly separated from the workspace background.
- Light palettes may be darker than the original defaults, but text remains in the light contrast model and panel surfaces should still read as elevated glass.
- Dark palettes should use low-saturation borders, layered translucency, and soft shadows instead of bright outlines.
- Background palettes must not override accent color or custom panel/widget color identity.
- Avoid pure white, washed-out pastel overload, cyberpunk neon, and glowing RGB edge effects.
